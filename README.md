# readme
A readme for Jonathan — evolving, but some values, ways that it goes wrong, and things that work well with me. 


## I hold myself to high standards
When I set my mind to doing something, I go all in. I will spend the time, effort, resources to do something well. 

_Slippery behavior_ This can lead to over-refinement, over-optimization, and sometimes coming across as a bit obsessed.

_Approaches that work well with me_

## I value differences of opinion and believe that these + working through them build better teams, projects, and software
Differences in approaches and ideas are how we learn, how we change our world view. I love working on things with people who don’t see the world the same way that I do. I like hearing conflicting approaches and opinions. I love to be proved wrong.

_Slippery behavior_ this isn’t so much a negative, but this disrupts the kinds of false agreement + agreeableness that our culture typically goes for. 

_Approaches that work well with me_

## I value action and impact over words
I would rather be doing something than talking about doing something. Consensus building is important, and I will do it, but if I could choose between doing and talking I would always choose doing.
 Combined with the previous value: if we disagree, I would rather take a few hours, build out proofs of concept for both ways and prove that one or the other way is a better way forward. 

_Slippery behavior_ Again, in combination with the above value: I grow tired very quickly of big debates that drag on. Having the same or similar conversation over and over without gathering more information makes me check out.

_Approaches that work well with me_ Take actions rather than explaining + convincing. Embodying disagree and commit.

## I am a helper
Pathologically, so. Whenever someone comes to me I immediately launch into (in my head at least) what can be done to resolve the situation.  

_Slippery behavior_ some people just want to vent! I try to check in and ask if someone is looking for help or a shoulder.

_Approaches that work well with me_ If you want to vent, lead with that.

## I take people at their word
I can be very literal. When someone says they want something, I will go with that until they say something different. I am quick to update explicit priors based on this.

_Slippery behavior_ I sometimes don’t check back in often enough, or I don’t update my approach with subtle nudges along the way.

_Approaches that work well with me_ Being upfront with what you want; tell me when that changes.

## I live for ambiguity and moving between levels
This is part of continuous learning, but I love living in ambiguity, the feeling of something that’s just fuzzy enough to not be able to see clearly, but there are enough details to know I’m going somewhere. I also love operating across time horizons and organizational scales: diving deep into one area to understand what’s going on, but then zooming out and figuring out how that connects with others things that the larger group (i.e. Posit as an organization) need + want.

_Slippery behavior_ assuming that others around me enjoy the freedom and opportunity in this ambiguity as much as I do.

_Approaches that work well with me_ being open about how much ambiguity you are comfortable with.
